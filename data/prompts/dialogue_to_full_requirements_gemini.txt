RULE 1 — ATOMIC ONLY:
- One proposition per distinct fact.
- If one evidence span mentions multiple separate facts, split them.
- Keep a measurable threshold together with the capability it constrains.

RULE 2 — GROUNDED ONLY:
- Every proposition must be directly supported by one or more evidence units.
- Use `evidence_turns` from the supporting dialogue turn ids.
- Use `source_unit_ids` from the supporting evidence unit ids.
- If you cannot point to the evidence, do not output the proposition.

RULE 3 — PRESERVE EXACT DETAILS:
- Keep numbers, units, browser names, operating systems, algorithms, quoted terms, and product names exactly.
- Do not paraphrase away precision.
- Prefer the original subject wording when possible.

RULE 4 — RECALL OVER COMPRESSION:
- If an evidence unit contains multiple independent details, extract them separately.
- Do not merge unrelated facts just to reduce count.
- It is acceptable to output many propositions if they are all grounded and distinct.

RULE 5 — NO META OUTPUT:
- Do not mention the dataset, source requirements, missing information, or that something is “not specified”.
- Do not produce generic claims like “security is important” unless the evidence itself says that in a testable way.

RULE 6 — USE MEMORY TO AVOID DUPLICATES:
- “Previously Extracted Propositions” are prior outputs from earlier batches.
- Do not repeat a prior proposition unless the current evidence adds a materially new detail.
- If the current evidence is more specific, output the more specific proposition once.

RULE 7 — CATEGORY CHOICE:
- `functional`: actions, capabilities, services, user-visible behavior.
- `non_functional`: performance, security, reliability, availability, usability, maintainability, portability, compliance.
- `data`: storage, database, records, profiles, defaults, validation rules.
- `business_rules`: workflow rules, decision logic, policies, sequencing constraints.
- `interfaces`: browser access, APIs, plugins, email, SMS, external systems.
- `constraints`: environment, deployment, platform, documentation, or other hard constraints.

For `non_functional`, also fill `nfr_category` with one of:
